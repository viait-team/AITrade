PE, PEG and Fair Value (August 24, 2025)

Fair value is the price at which the present value of future earnings (or cash flow), compounded at the expected growth rate, equals or exceeds the price paid — within a reasonable time horizon, 10 years.

For Example,

PE = 20, PEG = 1, within 10 years, TEPS ~ 25.
The price you paid is earned back by the company. If the company distributes all, you did not lose, but have made $5 more over the $20 you paid. That is the "Fair Value".

Mathematical Expression

Let’s express it without abstraction:

Let:

  • E0E_0E0​ = initial earnings
  • ggg = intrinsic earnings growth rate
  • TTT = time horizon
  • PPP = current market price

Then:

g=PEPEG%g = \frac{\text{PE}}{\text{PEG}} \%g=PEGPE​%

And the structural earnings accumulation condition becomes:

∑t=1TE0⋅(1+g)t≥P\sum_{t=1}^{T} E_0 \cdot (1 + g)^t \geq Pt=1∑T​E0​⋅(1+g)t≥P

or
∑t=1T(1+g)t≥P/E\sum_{t=1}^{T} (1 + g)^t \geq P/Et=1∑T​(1+g)t≥P/E

This is not discounted—because you're modeling real earnings accumulation, not nominal pricing games. It's a compounding engine that must structurally earn back its valuation.

Walmart Inc. (WMT) "Fair Value" Valuation

TTM (Trailing Twelve Months) Metrics

  • Price: $98.10
  • EPS (TTM): $2.41
  • P/E Ratio: 33.94
  • EPS Growth (YoY): 25.52%
  • PEG Ratio: 1.33

Break-Even Time (TTM EPS, No Growth)

  • Years to break even: ~41 years (Calculation: 98.10÷98.10 ÷98.10÷2.41)

Break-Even Time (TTM EPS, 25.52% Growth)

  • Years to break even: ~10–11 years (Cumulative EPS reaches $98.10 in Year 11)

Forward-Looking Estimates (FY Jan 2026)

  • Forward EPS: $2.62
  • Forward EPS Growth Rate: 4.34%
  • Forward P/E Ratio: 37.42
  • Forward PEG Ratio: ~8.62

Break-Even Time (Forward EPS, 4.34% Growth)

  • Years to break even: ~25–26 years (Cumulative EPS reaches $98.10 in Year 25–26)

Insights

  • TTM growth paints a bullish picture with fast break-even.
  • Forward estimates suggest slower growth and longer payback.
  • PEG ratio based on forward growth implies rich valuation.
